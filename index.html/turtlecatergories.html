Turtle categories

Turtle categories in Sri lanka

Out of the seven living sea turtle species recorded in the world, Sri Lanka is blessed to have five species reported along the beautiful coastal belt of the island - coming for nesting. The Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas), Olive Ridley (Lepidochelys olivacea), Hawksbill (Eretmochelys imbricata), Loggerhead (Caretta caretta) and Leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea) make the big five. So if you plan a beach holiday in Sri Lanka, with the right experts like Mahoora Tented Camps Sri Lanka, you are more than likely bound to see a few! The Indian Ocean contains the tropical island of Sri Lanka. Five different kinds of sea turtles live there, and they play a significant role in the nation's marine ecosystem. The physical qualities, habitat, and behavior of these sea turtles are used to classify them. The five types of turtles that can be found in Sri Lanka will be covered in this article.


1. The Olive Ridley Turtle




Sri Lanka, the Olive Ridley Turtle is the most prevalent species of sea turtle. They have a characteristic heart-shaped shell and are well recognized for their synchronized nesting habit, in which hundreds of females come ashore to lay their eggs at the same time. Being omnivores, Olive Ridley Turtles eat a variety of marine creatures, such as jellyfish, crabs, and shrimp. They are located in shallow waters off Sri Lanka's coast, where you can watch them soaking up the sun.

2.Green Turtle




Green Turtle is a sizable marine turtle with a shell that is smooth and olive in color. They are herbivores and eat algae and seagrass. Green turtles are well-known for their protracted ocean journeys and can be seen in both shallow and deep waters near Sri Lanka. They frequently come to the surface for breath or sit in the sun on rocky outcrops.

3.Hawksbill Turtle




Sea turtles from Sri Lanka include the highly endangered Hawksbill Turtle. They stand out for their unusual pointed beaks and exquisitely patterned shells, which are highly prized for their decorative qualities. Sponges and other invertebrates are the primary food source for hawksbill turtles in coral reefs. They are primarily discovered in shallow waters around coral reefs in Sri Lanka.

4.leatherback turtle




a maximum length of 2 meters and a maximum weight of 900 kg, the leatherback turtle is the largest species of marine turtle in the world. They have an unusual leathery shell with tiny scales on it. In the deep waters of Sri Lanka, leatherback turtles can be seen. They devour jellyfish. They are renowned for making lengthy migrations across the ocean, and you can frequently observe them soaking in the rays on the water's surface.

5. Loggerhead Turtle




A huge sea turtle with a broad, flattened head and a reddish-brown shell is called a loggerhead turtle. They can be found in shallow and deep waters around Sri Lanka, where they prey on a range of marine animals like crabs, shrimp, and mollusks. The strong swimmers known as loggerhead turtles are frequently spotted surfacing for air or soaking up the sun on rocky ledges.

The marine environment of Sri Lanka is significantly reliant on sea turtles. The physical traits, habitats, and behaviors of the five species present in Sri Lanka are used to classify them. The most prevalent species of turtle in Sri Lanka is the Olive Ridley Turtle, however the Hawksbill Turtle is seriously endangered. The government has adopted rules and regulations as well as the activities of non-governmental organizations (NGOs) to conserve these turtles and their habitats. For the enjoyment of future generations, it is crucial to keep up these efforts to safeguard the survival of these lovely creatures.
